- Empty/incomplete GBP = red flag for international clients

- Centralized location for client testimonials

- Better than scattered Trustpilot/Clutch reviews

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Don't Do This:

1. **Keyword Stuffing**:

   - Bad: "Devaland Klaviyo Expert Klaviyo Agency Klaviyo Automation Klaviyo Email"
   - Good: "Devaland - Klaviyo Email Marketing & Automation Agency"

2. **Service Area Confusion**:

   - Don't set local radius (irrelevant for international B2B)
   - Don't select "I serve customers at their location" (causes confusion)

3. **Ignoring Reviews**:

   - Respond to ALL reviews within 24-48 hours
   - Never delete or hide negative reviews (respond professionally)

4. **Inconsistent NAP** (Name, Address, Phone):

   - Ensure exact match across: GBP, website footer, about page, contact page
   - Inconsistency hurts SEO and trust

5. **Neglecting Posts**:

   - Don't let GBP go stale
   - Post at least 2x per month minimum

6. **Poor Quality Photos**:

   - No blurry, pixelated, or unprofessional images
   - All photos should be branded and high-resolution

7. **Fake Reviews**:
   - Never buy reviews or ask friends/employees to review
   - Google detects and penalizes this severely

## FAQs for International B2B Companies

**Q: Does GBP help if I don't serve local customers?**

A: Yes! GBP serves different purposes for international B2B:

- Trust signal for international prospects researching you
- SEO boost (backlink from high-authority Google property)
- Review aggregation platform
- Brand legitimacy indicator
- Appears in branded search results

**Q: Should I hide my Romanian address?**

A: No! Having a physical address is a positive trust signal. International clients expect European agencies to have EU addresses. Hiding it looks suspicious. Instead, clearly state "Remote services for USA, UK, CA, AU, EU clients" in your description.

**Q: Why are my profile views so low?**

A: For international B2B, GBP isn't a primary discovery channel. Views come from:

- Clients researching you after finding website
- People verifying your legitimacy
- Branded searches (your company name)
- Focus on quality over quantity - 72 views from qualified prospects > 500 views from local residents

**Q: Should I respond to Romanian-language inquiries?**

A: Yes, but quickly direct to target market:

- Respond politely in Romanian
- Explain you specialize in international e-commerce
- Refer local inquiries to local agencies (goodwill gesture)
- Most inquiries should come through website anyway

**Q: Can I run ads to my GBP?**

A: Not recommended for international B2B. Better to:

- Run Google Ads to website landing pages
- Use LinkedIn Ads for B2B targeting
- Invest in SEO for organic visibility
- GBP is credibility tool, not acquisition channel
